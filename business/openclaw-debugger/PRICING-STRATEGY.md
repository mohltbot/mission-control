# Pricing Strategy & Psychology

## Core Principle
Price based on VALUE, not time. A 30-minute fix that saves 3 hours of debugging is worth $75+.

---

## Pricing Tiers

### One-Time Fixes

**Basic Fix — $75**
- Single configuration issue
- 24-hour delivery
- Text explanation
- Perfect for: Clear, isolated errors

**Standard Fix — $150**
- Up to 3 related issues
- 12-hour delivery
- Detailed documentation
- 7-day follow-up
- Perfect for: Complex configs, multiple errors

**Emergency Fix — $250**
- 2-hour delivery guaranteed
- Video walkthrough
- 30-day email support
- Skip-the-queue priority
- Perfect for: Production down, demo tomorrow

### Monthly Retainers

**Monitoring — $300/month**
- Weekly config health checks
- 3 fixes included
- Priority support (12-hour response)
- Prevent issues before they break
- Perfect for: Active builders with multiple agents

**Peace of Mind — $500/month**
- Daily health monitoring
- Unlimited fixes
- 6-hour response guarantee
- Monthly optimization report
- Perfect for: Agencies, businesses with revenue at stake

**Enterprise — $1,000/month**
- Dedicated Slack channel
- 2-hour response guarantee
- Custom configurations
- Team training sessions
- Quarterly strategy reviews
- Perfect for: Teams, high-stakes deployments

## Pricing Psychology Tactics

### 1. Anchoring
**Show high first:**
> "Enterprise clients pay $1,000/month. For individual builders, I offer $150 per fix or $300/month monitoring."

**Why it works:** $300 feels reasonable next to $1,000.

### 2. Middle Option Bias
**Make Standard the obvious choice:**
- Basic: $75 (too limited)
- Standard: $150 ⭐ POPULAR (just right)
- Premium: $300 (maybe too much)

**Why it works:** People avoid extremes. Middle = safe choice.

### 3. Urgency Pricing
**Emergency premium:**
> "Standard delivery: $150 (24 hours)  
> Emergency delivery: $250 (2 hours)"

**Why it works:** Pain of downtime > cost of premium.

### 4. Subscription Framing
**Cost vs. value:**
> "You just spent 4 hours debugging. At your rate, that's $400.  
> My fix: $75 in 30 minutes."

**Why it works:** Reframes $75 as cheap vs. their time cost.

### 5. Risk Reversal
**Money-back guarantee:**
> "Fixed or free. No questions asked."

**Why it works:** Removes risk. They only pay if it works.

## Pricing Evolution

### Phase 1: Proof of Concept (Weeks 1-2)
**Goal:** Get 5 reviews
- Price: $25-50 per fix
- Strategy: Undercut market, over-deliver
- Target: Early adopters, price-sensitive

### Phase 2: Market Rate (Weeks 3-8)
**Goal:** Build recurring revenue
- Price: $75-150 per fix, $300/month
- Strategy: Raise after reviews, add retainer
- Target: Regular users, agencies

### Phase 3: Premium (Months 3-6)
**Goal:** Maximize profit per customer
- Price: $150-300 per fix, $500-1000/month
- Strategy: Add enterprise tier, case studies
- Target: Businesses, high-stakes users

### Phase 4: Scale (Months 6+)
**Goal:** Productize, hire, systematize
- Price: Keep premium, add productized services
- Strategy: Tools, templates, team
- Target: Volume + premium mix

## Package Design

### The "Good-Better-Best" Framework

**Good — $75 (Basic)**
- Gets them in the door
- Limited scope manages expectations
- Upsell path to Standard

**Better — $150 (Standard)** ⭐
- Most popular (mark it!)
- Complete solution
- Sweet spot of value/price

**Best — $300 (Premium)**
- Anchors high
- Captures price-insensitive customers
- Makes Standard look reasonable

### The Decoy Effect
**Add a decoy to nudge toward your target:**

Option A: $75 (Basic)
Option B: $150 (Standard) ← Target
Option C: $400 (Emergency 1 hour)

**Why it works:** $150 looks reasonable vs. $400.

## Handling Price Objections

### "That's expensive"

**Reframe the cost:**
> "You just spent 4 hours debugging. At $100/hour, that's $400 of your time. I fix it in 30 minutes for $75. You're actually saving $325."

**Compare to alternatives:**
> "Hiring a dev to learn OpenClaw: $2,000+  
> Figuring it out yourself: 6+ hours  
> My fix: $75, 30 minutes"

**Offer DIY help:**
> "Happy to point you in the right direction if you want to DIY. But if you just want it fixed, I'm $75."

### "Can you do it for less?"

**Stand firm:**
> "I don't discount — I deliver fast, guaranteed fixes. The price reflects the value of getting you unstuck quickly."

**Offer less scope:**
> "I can do $50 for a diagnosis + DIY guide. But the full fix is $75."

**Create urgency:**
> "I'm raising prices to $100 next week. Lock in $75 if we start today."

### "I'll think about it"

**Add urgency:**
> "Fair enough. I'm booking up — if you want it fixed this week, I have 2 slots left."

**Reduce friction:**
> "Tell you what — I'll look at your error for free. If I can fix it, you decide if it's worth $75. Deal?"

**Follow up:**
> "Still stuck on that OpenClaw issue? Happy to help when you're ready."

## Revenue Math

### Target: $5,000/month

**Option A: All One-Time Fixes**
- 34 fixes at $150 = $5,100
- ~8 fixes/week
- High volume, always hunting

**Option B: Hybrid (Recommended)**
- 10 monthly retainers at $300 = $3,000
- 14 one-time fixes at $150 = $2,100
- Total: $5,100
- ~4 retainers + 3-4 fixes/week

**Option C: Mostly Recurring**
- 15 monthly retainers at $300 = $4,500
- 4 emergency fixes at $250 = $1,000
- Total: $5,500
- Predictable + upside

**Winner:** Option B or C. Recurring revenue = stability.

## Pricing Principles

1. **Price is a signal** — Higher prices = higher perceived value
2. **Anchor high** — Show expensive option first
3. **Middle sells** — Make your target the middle option
4. **Guarantee removes risk** — "Fixed or free" converts better
5. **Raise prices regularly** — Every 5-10 customers, raise 20%
6. **Subscriptions scale** — Prioritize monthly over one-time
7. **Enterprise pays more** — Don't leave money on table

---

## When to Raise Prices

### Raise When:
- [ ] You have 5+ reviews
- [ ] You're at 80% capacity
- [ ] Customers say "that's it?"
- [ ] You've added new skills/services
- [ ] It's been 1 month

### How to Raise:
1. Announce 1 week in advance
2. Honor old price for existing inquiries
3. Grandfather current retainer clients
4. Test new price with 20% of traffic

---

## Final Pricing Advice

**Don't compete on price.** Compete on:
- Speed (30 minutes vs. hours)
- Reliability (100% success rate)
- Convenience (they don't learn OpenClaw)
- Peace of mind (monitoring prevents issues)

**You're not selling a config fix.** You're selling:
- Time saved
- Frustration avoided
- Agents that work
- Sleep at night

**Price accordingly.**
