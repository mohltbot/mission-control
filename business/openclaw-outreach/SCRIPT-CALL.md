# 15-Minute Discovery Call Script

> Use this to run a tight, valuable call that converts

---

## Pre-Call Checklist (2 min)

- [ ] Review their LinkedIn profile and company website
- [ ] Note any recent posts or company news
- [ ] Prepare 2-3 specific questions based on their industry
- [ ] Have your calendar open for booking next steps
- [ ] Test your audio/video

---

## Opening (2 min)

**Goal:** Build rapport, set expectations

**Script:**

> "Thanks for taking the time, [Name]. I promised this would be 15 minutes and I'll stick to that. Here's what I'd love to do: first, understand what you're dealing with day-to-day, then share how I've helped similar businesses—if it makes sense, we can talk about next steps. Sound good?"

**Then:**
> "Before we dive in—how's business going overall?"

*(Listen for stress signals, growth, challenges)*

---

## Discovery Questions (6 min)

**Goal:** Identify pain points and quantify the cost

### Opening Question
> "Walk me through a typical week. What are you personally spending time on that feels like it should be handled by now?"

### Follow-Up Questions (pick 2-3 based on their answers):

**Time & Tasks:**
- "How many hours a week would you estimate you spend on [specific task they mentioned]?"
- "What tasks keep you at the office later than you'd like to be?"
- "If you had an extra 5 hours a week, what would you focus on?"

**Current Solutions:**
- "How are you handling [task] right now?"
- "Have you tried automating anything before? What happened?"
- "What's stopped you from solving this so far?"

**Pain & Cost:**
- "What happens if this doesn't get fixed?"
- "How much is this costing you—in time, money, or sanity?"
- "Who else is this affecting? Your team? Family?"

**Decision Making:**
- "When you've made changes like this before, how did that process work?"
- "Besides you, who else would need to be involved in a decision like this?"

### Listen For These Pain Signals:

| What They Say | What It Means |
|---------------|---------------|
| "I'm drowning in emails" | Inbox automation opportunity |
| "I work too many hours" | Multiple workflow automations |
| "I can't find good help" | Automation replaces hiring |
| "My team is overwhelmed" | Scale without adding headcount |
| "I hate technology" | Needs simple, done-for-you solution |
| "I tried [tool] but..." | Failed DIY, needs implementation help |

---

## The Pitch (3 min)

**Goal:** Position OpenClaw as the solution to their specific pain

### Transition:
> "Based on what you're telling me, it sounds like [summarize their top 2 pain points]. Is that right?"

### The Pitch Framework:

**1. Similar Story:**
> "I worked with a [similar business] owner who was dealing with the exact same thing. They were spending [X hours] on [task] every week."

**2. What We Did:**
> "We set up a simple automation that handles [specific tasks]. Nothing fancy—just reliable systems that work in the background."

**3. The Result:**
> "Within the first month, they got back [X hours] a week and [specific business impact—more clients, less stress, etc.]"

**4. How It Works (Keep It Simple):**
> "Here's how it works: I set up OpenClaw automation tailored to your business. It handles the repetitive stuff—like [their specific pain points]—so you don't have to think about it. You get weekly check-ins to make sure it's working, and I'm available when you need tweaks."

**5. The Offer:**
> "I'd propose we start with a 2-week pilot focused on [their top pain point]. You see results before committing to anything long-term."

---

## Pricing Conversation (1 min)

**Goal:** Introduce pricing naturally, gauge reaction

### Script:

> "Let me be transparent about investment. Most clients start with a setup fee of $[X] and then $[Y]/month for ongoing management and support. For that, you get [specific deliverables]."

> "Given what we talked about—[their pain point] costing you [time/money]—does that range feel reasonable to explore?"

### If They Hesitate:

> "Totally understand. What part of that feels off—budget, or not sure about the value yet?"

*(Address the specific concern)*

### Pricing Framework Options:

| Package | Setup | Monthly | Best For |
|---------|-------|---------|----------|
| Starter | $500 | $200 | 1-2 simple automations |
| Growth | $1,500 | $500 | 3-5 workflows + ongoing support |
| Custom | Custom quote | Custom | Complex multi-system setups |

---

## Next Steps & Close (1 min)

**Goal:** Get commitment to next step

### Script:

> "Here's what I suggest as a next step: I'll send you a brief proposal outlining exactly what we'd automate for you, the timeline, and investment. You can review it, ask questions, and decide if it makes sense. No pressure either way."

**If they're interested:**
> "Great. I'll have that to you by [day]. Should we schedule a quick 20-min call for [day] to walk through it together?"

**If they're hesitant:**
> "Fair enough. Can I send you a one-page case study of a similar business so you can see exactly what this looks like? Then you can decide if you want to keep talking."

### Always End With:

> "Whether we work together or not, I hope this call gave you some ideas for tackling [their pain point]. Feel free to reach out anytime."

---

## Post-Call Actions (5 min)

1. **Send follow-up within 2 hours**
2. **Include:** Summary of what you discussed, proposal or case study, calendar link
3. **Update your tracking sheet** with notes and next steps
4. **Set reminder** for follow-up if you don't hear back

## Red Flags (When to Walk Away)

- Wants everything for free or "exposure"
- Can't articulate their problem clearly
- Keeps asking for features you don't offer
- Expects instant results with no effort
- Has gone through 5+ consultants in 2 years
- Won't commit to a next step after 2 follow-ups

**Script:**
> "Based on what I'm hearing, I might not be the right fit for what you need. I'd recommend [alternative solution]. Happy to stay in touch if things change."

---

## Success Metrics to Track

- **Call-to-proposal rate:** Target 70%+
- **Proposal-to-close rate:** Target 30%+
- **Average deal size:** Track and optimize
- **Time from first call to close:** Target <2 weeks

---

## Pro Tips

1. **Take notes during the call** - they'll appreciate you remembering details
2. **Use their words back to them** - if they say "drowning," use "drowning" in your pitch
3. **Silence is okay** - let them think after you present pricing
4. **End early if possible** - finishing in 12 minutes builds trust
5. **Send the proposal fast** - while the pain is fresh in their mind
